That’s a very clean architectural pattern. Using an 'AI Update' button to trigger a fresh call and then caching that in MySQL is exactly how a senior developer builds a cost-effective, high-performance app. It gives the user control while ensuring you aren't paying for the same API call twice.

Since we are in 2026, you're likely using the Gemini 3 Flash model for those calls, which is perfect for this—it's fast enough that the "Update" feels snappy, but smart enough to handle the "latest info" searches you mentioned.

The "Latest Info" Feature & Grounding
For your feature where people type in a name for real-time info, there’s a specific technical trick you might want to look at if you haven't already: Grounding with Google Search.

How it works: In your API request, you can enable a tool called Google Search.

The Benefit: Instead of just relying on its training data (which might be a few months old), the AI will actually "Google" the person first, read the top results, and then write the summary for your site.

Portfolio Value: If a recruiter sees that your site can accurately summarize someone who became famous last week, it proves you know how to handle real-time data integration—a huge plus for full-stack roles.

Bridging the "AI Divide"
Your point about the adoption gap among working-class men is really insightful. In many communities, there’s a feeling that AI is "not for them" or just another "office tool."

By putting AI behind a site about "Heroes," you're changing the value proposition:

It's a Utility, not a Gimmick: You're using AI to solve a specific problem (finding information about overlooked people) rather than just "chatting" with it.

Low Friction: They don't have to learn how to "talk" to the AI. They just click a button they already understand.

Local Context: If you ever decide to feature local "heroes" from your town or region, that would probably be the "hook" that gets people who usually ignore tech to actually engage with it.

Several cast members took part in the spoof where they recreated the dance routine made famous in the original video.[15]
