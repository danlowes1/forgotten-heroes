Billy the Fish is one of Viz comic's most enduring and beloved characters, first appearing in the iconic British adult comic in the 1980s. He is the star goalkeeper for the perpetually struggling AFC Fulchester, a fictional football team that never seems to win a match without Billy's extraordinary interventions. Born to a Geordie fishmonger father and a mother who was, in fact, a haddock (she'd been caught off the Tyne during a particularly rough storm and, against all odds, survived the filleting process long enough to fall in love), Billy's piscine heritage grants him uncanny abilities on the pitch, such as flapping about in the goalmouth like a cod on a slab while somehow blocking shots that would baffle a human with actual arms. His teammates, a motley crew of inept locals including the chain-smoking manager Grim Jim and the perennially injured striker Shakin' Stevens (no relation), rely on Billy's floundering saves to scrape through games, often against rivals like the posh lads from Poshchester United or the undead squad from Zombie Rovers. In one memorable strip, Billy heroically swallows the ball whole during a penalty shootout, only to regurgitate it into the opponent's net after a bout of indigestion brought on by pre-match kippers. Critics have hailed him as a metaphor for the working-class underdog, though really he's just a fish in shorts, proving that in British football, even the halibut can have its day.
