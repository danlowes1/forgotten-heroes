Norbot - The Reluctant Robot

Wallace and Gromit: Vengeance Most Fowl 2024

Norbot is a highly advanced, multi-armed service robot created by the brilliant but scatterbrained inventor Wallace in the latest Wallace and Gromit adventure, Vengeance Most Fowl.

Built to handle all of Wallace's domestic and inventing chores, Norbot is programmed for efficiency but often displays a weary, long-suffering personality when dealing with his creator's chaotic demands.

Creation and Function

Built in Wallace's basement workshop at 62 West Wallaby Street, Norbot represents a significant leap in Wallace's robotic technology. He is equipped with multiple telescopic arms with various tool attachments, allowing him to cook, clean, garden, and assist with complex engineering projects.

"I've finally done it, Gromit! A true mechanical marvel! Now, about that cup of tea..."
- Wallace upon activating Norbot

Despite his advanced programming, Norbot often seems to possess a soul, frequently sighing with mechanical exasperation or pausing in silent disbelief at Wallace's latest harebrained scheme.

Role in Vengeance Most Fowl

Norbot's primary function is to manage Wallace's new "Smart-Garden" but quickly becomes indispensable to the household. His efficiency is both a blessing and a curse, as it allows Wallace to be even more idle and dream up more troublesome inventions.

When Feathers McGraw returns to seek vengeance, Norbot becomes a crucial asset in defending West Wallaby Street, using his many arms and tools in ways Wallace never anticipated.

Legacy and Character

Norbot perfectly continues the Aardman tradition of giving technology very human personalities. He is the latest in a line of mechanical helpers that includes the iconic rocket-launching bed from A Grand Day Out and the automated trousers from The Wrong Trousers.

"Norbot is that feeling of exasperated competence we all have when dealing with a well-meaning but hopelessly disorganized boss. He's all of us."
- Animation Film Review

Norbot stands as a testament to the idea that the most advanced robot is not always the one with the most weapons, but sometimes the one with the most patience.
