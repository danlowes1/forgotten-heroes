Gillian De Terville: The Tempting Replacement

The Page 3 Pioneer Who Stole Heaven 17's Spotlight

Who Was Gillian De Terville?

Gillian De Terville made history as the first black Page 3 model in British tabloid history, posing topless for The Sun at the tender age of 21 in a move that turned heads and raised eyebrows across the nation's breakfast tables. But her crowning glory—or perhaps her most lip-smacking substitution—came when she stepped in as the emergency replacement for Carol Kenyon in Heaven 17's 1983 "Temptation" video, after the actual singer threw a wobbly over her appearance fee and decided to tempt fate by staying home.

Gillian, a dead ringer for Kenyon with her sultry gaze and impeccable timing, mouthed the powerhouse vocals with such hypnotic flair that fans were left wondering if the synth-pop siren had been cloned in a Sheffield lab, her every "higher and higher" luring viewers into a trance like a budget Bond villainess in shoulder pads.

Born to a South London cabbie father and a mother who was, in fact, a mythical succubus (she'd been summoned during a dodgy séance at a glam modelling agency and stuck around for the free vol-au-vents), Gillian's innate ability to tempt and tease made her the perfect stand-in, flapping her lashes in the video's dimly lit nightclub scenes while Glenn Gregory warbled on about souls for sale.

The Great Video Swap

In one absurd cut, she reportedly ad-libbed a wink that caused the camera operator to drop his rig, delaying filming by a full day and inflating the budget to levels that would tempt even the most frugal producer to tears. Critics at the time hailed her as a symbol of 80s excess, though really she was just a Page 3 pioneer proving that when the original bows out, the replacement can steal the show—leaving Kenyon to ponder what might have been from the comfort of her unfilmed sofa.

Fun Facts About Gillian's Tempting Talents

  • Could mime "higher and higher" while simultaneously judging your life choices
  • Her shoulder pads were so sharp they counted as defensive weapons
  • Once tempted a camera operator into dropping his equipment with a single wink

From Page 3 to Pop Stardom

After the video wrapped, Gillian parlayed her tempting talents into acting gigs, including a blink-and-you'll-miss-it Bond girl role in Octopussy, where she tempted Roger Moore with nothing more than a knowing glance and a cocktail umbrella.

"Gillian didn't just replace Carol Kenyon; she upgraded the entire temptation package with better hair and more intimidating eyeliner." – Synth-Pop Historian

Her transition from tabloid sensation to music video siren to Bond girl marked one of the most unexpected career trajectories of the 1980s, proving that sometimes being in the right place with the right cheekbones could open more doors than any formal training.

The Legacy of a Last-Minute Legend

Gillian De Terville's story serves as a testament to the power of being ready when opportunity knocks—even if that opportunity involves lip-syncing to someone else's vocals while dressed like a corporate dominatrix. Her brief but memorable turn in the "Temptation" video became iconic not despite its last-minute casting, but because of it.

"She was the human equivalent of finding a fiver in an old jacket – completely unexpected but thoroughly welcome." – 80s Music Video Connoisseur

While Carol Kenyon's powerful vocals drove the song, Gillian's hypnotic screen presence gave it a visual identity that would define the track for generations of viewers who never knew there'd been a replacement at all.

Beyond the Video: A Cultural Pioneer

Gillian's impact extended far beyond that one music video. As the first black Page 3 model, she broke barriers in British tabloid culture during an era when such representation was virtually unheard of. Her presence in both the tabloids and the music video scene represented a quiet revolution in 1980s Britain.

Gillian's Greatest Moments

  • Pioneering diversity in British tabloids before it was fashionable
  • Proving that sometimes the understudy outshines the star
  • Making shoulder pads look both intimidating and irresistible
  • Tempting Roger Moore with nothing but a cocktail umbrella

From newsprint to music television to the silver screen, Gillian De Terville carved a path that few could have predicted, proving that sometimes the most memorable performances come from the most unexpected replacements.
