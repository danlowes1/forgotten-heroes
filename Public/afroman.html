Afroman - The Philosopher of Stoner Rap

Music 1974-Present

Afroman (Joseph Foreman) is the cult-favorite rapper whose humorous, weed-infused storytelling created some of the most quotable lyrics in hip-hop history. Best known for his 2001 hit "Because I Got High", his music blends street wisdom with absurdist comedy, often overlooked by mainstream rap despite his lasting influence on stoner culture.

Lyrical Genius in the Margins

Afroman's career is built on unexpectedly profound party anthems:

  • Self-produced his breakout hit in his cousin's basement with $200
  • Created a subgenre blending hip-hop, blues, and stoner comedy
  • Turned viral fame into a 20+ year independent career
  • First rapper to make the Billboard charts with harmonica solos
Colt 45 and two zig zags, baby that's all we need
We can go to the park after dark, smoke that tumbleweed
- Afroman, "Colt 45" (2000)

Forgotten Lyricism

Beneath the party surface, Afroman's lyrics reveal surprising depth:

1. "Because I Got High" (2001)

"I was gonna clean my room until I got high
I was gonna get up and find the broom but then I got high
My room is still messed up and I know why (why man?)
Because I got high, because I got high, because I got high"

A deceptively brilliant satire of addiction cycles, wrapped in a novelty hit.

2. "Crazy Rap" (2000)

"I live in the woods with my cousin named Sam
We got fake IDs and a beat-up old van
We play Nintendo and smoke a lil' grass
And drink Olde English until we fall on our ass"

Blue-collar poetry that predates "trailer rap" by a decade.

3. "Palmdale" (2001)

"I left my wallet in El Segundo
I gotta get it, I got got to
It had my money, my bus pass
And the phone numbers of all the fine hos in the class"

An epic stoner Odyssey about retracing your steps.

Why Was He Forgotten?

Several factors contributed to Afroman's cult status:

  • Novelty label: Critics dismissed him as a one-hit wonder
  • Independent hustle: Rejected major labels to keep creative control
  • No filter: Lyrics too raw for commercial rap's polished image
  • Weed culture: Mainstream hip-hop ignored stoner rap until recently

Rediscovery and Legacy

In recent years, Afroman's influence has grown:

  • 2008: "Because I Got High" added to the National Archives as cultural artifact
  • 2016: Performed at Coachella's cannabis-themed stage
  • Influence: Paved way for artists like Lil Dicky and Yung Gravy
  • Modern parallels: His DIY approach mirrors SoundCloud rap's rise
