Frank Worthington - The Maverick Who Scored a Wonder Goal

Sports April 21, 1979

Francis "Frank" Worthington (23 November 1948 – 22 March 2021) was an English professional footballer who played as a forward. Known for his flamboyant style, both on and off the pitch, he represented England eight times.

Worthington was a true maverick of the English game, but he is arguably best remembered for an audacious piece of skill while playing for Bolton Wanderers against Ipswich Town in 1979—a goal that encapsulated his brilliance and carefree approach to football.

The Moment of Genius

On April 21, 1979, Bolton Wanderers faced Ipswich Town. With the score tied, Worthington received the ball with his back to goal on the edge of the penalty area. What followed was a moment of sublime, unexpected brilliance.

"I was thinking: 'Do a one-two, Frank, or just lay it off.' But I was on a high. I was trying to do things that hadn't been done before."
- Frank Worthington

Worthington flicked the ball up over the head of defender Allan Hunter, spun, chested the ball down, and then volleyed it past the despairing dive of goalkeeper Paul Cooper. It was a goal of such dazzling skill that it has lived on in the highlight reels, often hailed as one of the greatest goals in First Division history.

A Unique Legacy

Worthington's career was a journey across over 20 clubs, but the wonder goal cemented his place as a true showman. He was often compared to George Best for his flair, charisma, and occasionally rebellious spirit, which arguably overshadowed his considerable talent.

While his individual accolades are few, his influence on the game as a colourful, skillful entertainer is undeniable. After his playing days, Worthington often recalled the Ipswich goal, noting that it was the perfect embodiment of his love for the beautiful game played with panache.

He was one of the great showmen of the English game during the 1970s; dubbed “the working man’s George Best”, he was as well-known for his uninhibited enjoyment of life off the pitch as he was for the skill he showed gracing it for two decades.
