Bob Haro - The Father of Freestyle BMX

Action Sports 2025

Robert "Bob" Haro (born June 29, 1958) is an American former professional freestyle BMX rider, factory rider, and entrepreneur.

He is widely credited as being the "Father of Freestyle BMX." Haro's innovative riding style and creative vision in the late 1970s and early 1980s transformed the sport from a racing-focused discipline to a global phenomenon of tricks and expression. He not only pioneered many of the foundational tricks, but also founded Haro Bikes, one of the most influential brands in BMX history.

The Historic Moment

While there isn't one single moment like a game-winning goal, Bob Haro's "historic moment" was a series of creative breakthroughs. In the early 1980s, he performed and popularized tricks like the "bunny hop," "endo," and "tailwhip," which were entirely new concepts on a bicycle.

"Freestyle was a natural evolution. We were kids who loved to ride our bikes, and we started doing things on them that weren't for racing. We were just having fun and being creative."
- Bob Haro

Haro's innovative spirit led him to found the BMX Action Trick Team, which toured the country performing demonstrations and bringing the sport to a wider audience. This effort, combined with his visionary bike designs, laid the groundwork for freestyle BMX as we know it today.

Aftermath and Legacy

Despite his monumental influence, Bob Haro's name is not always as well-known to the general public as some later-generation action sports stars. However, his legacy is deeply woven into the fabric of BMX culture. He designed the first BMX race plate and went on to create some of the most iconic freestyle BMX bikes of all time, including the Haro Freestyler.

Haro's contributions changed the world of cycling and inspired countless riders to push the boundaries of what was possible on two wheels. He is a true, albeit often forgotten, hero of action sports, whose influence continues to resonate with every trick performed today.
